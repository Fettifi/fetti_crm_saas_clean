Fetti Dictate for Outlook

Speak a rough note and get a polished, professional email — written and inserted right into your Outlook draft.

How to use it (after installing)

  1. Start a new email or reply in Outlook.
  2. On the ribbon/toolbar, click Fetti Dictate to open the side panel.
  3. Tap Record and just talk — or type the gist. Pick a tone.
  4. Hit “Write professional email,” review, then Insert into email.

Install it once

Use the file Fetti placed on your Desktop: Fetti-Outlook-Dictate.xml. Pick your Outlook below.

  1. Open Outlook and start a new email.
  2. On the message ribbon, click Apps (or … More → Get Add-ins).
  3. Choose My add-ins → scroll to Custom Add-ins → Add a custom add-in → Add from File…
  4. Select ~/Desktop/Fetti-Outlook-Dictate.xml and confirm Install.
  5. Close and reopen the compose window — Fetti Dictate appears on the ribbon.

If you don’t see “Apps,” update Outlook, or use Outlook on the web (next tab) — same account, same result.

  1. Go to outlook.office.com and click New mail.
  2. In the compose window, click Apps (top toolbar) → Get Add-ins.
  3. My add-ins → Custom Add-ins → Add a custom add-in → Add from File…
  4. Upload Fetti-Outlook-Dictate.xml and confirm.
  5. Reopen a draft — Fetti Dictate is in the Apps menu.
  1. Open Outlook, Home → Get Add-ins (or All Apps → Add-ins).
  2. My add-ins → Custom Addins → Add a custom add-in → Add from File…
  3. Pick Fetti-Outlook-Dictate.xml → Install.
  4. Start a new email — Fetti Dictate shows on the Message ribbon.

About the microphone

The first time you record, your browser/Outlook may ask to allow the microphone — click Allow. If recording isn’t available in your version, you can still type the note (or use macOS dictation: press the 🎤 key or Fn Fn) and Fetti will polish it.
